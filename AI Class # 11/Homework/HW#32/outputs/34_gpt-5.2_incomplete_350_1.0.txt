=== PROMPT ===
Explain the concept of 'temperature' in LLM generation.

=== RAW OUTPUT ===
In LLM text generation, **temperature** is a parameter that controls how *random* (vs. *deterministic*) the model’s next-token choices are.

## What it acts on
At each step, the model produces a probability distribution over possible next tokens. Temperature rescales that distribution **before sampling**:

- **Low temperature (< 1.0):** makes the distribution *sharper* → the highest‑probability tokens become even more likely.
- **High temperature (> 1.0):** makes the distribution *flatter* → lower‑probability tokens become more competitive.

Mathematically, if a model outputs logits \(z_i\), temperature \(T\) is applied as:
\[
p_i = \frac{e^{z_i/T}}{\sum_j e^{z_j/T}}
\]
- \(T \to 0\): approaches “always pick the best token” (greedy behavior).
- \(T = 1\): unchanged from the model’s native distribution.
- Larger \(T\): more exploratory sampling.

## Practical effects
- **Temperature = 0 (or very close):** most consistent, repeatable outputs; good for factual or format-sensitive tasks, but can be repetitive or get “stuck” in common phrasing.
- **Temperature ~ 0.7–1.0:** balanced—still coherent, with some variation.
- **Temperature ~ 1.2–2.0:** more diverse/creative but higher risk of errors, incoherence, or hallucinations.
