The relationship between **tokens** and **billing** is central to how most AI language models (like OpenAI's GPT models) charge for usage. Here’s a clear explanation:

---

### What is a Token?

- A **token** is a chunk of text. It can be as short as one character or as long as one word (e.g., "a", "apple", "running").
- For English, 1 token ≈ 4 characters or 0.75 words on average.
- Example: The sentence "ChatGPT is great!" is **5 tokens**: ["Chat", "G", "PT", " is", " great!"]

---

### How Tokens Relate to Billing

- **Billing is based on the number of tokens processed.**
- Every time you send a prompt or receive a response, the total number of tokens (input + output) is counted.
- **You are charged per token** according to the pricing of the model you use (e.g., GPT-3.5, GPT-4, etc.).
