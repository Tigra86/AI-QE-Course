Tokenization is the process of converting a piece of text into smaller units called tokens. These tokens can be words, phrases, or even characters, depending on the application. Here's a step-by-step overview of the tokenization process:

1. **Input Text**: 
   - Begin with a block of text that you want to tokenize. This could be a sentence, paragraph, or entire document.

2. **Preprocessing**:
   - **Normalization**: Convert the text to a consistent format, such as lowercasing all letters to ensure uniformity.
   - **Cleaning**: Remove unwanted characters, such as punctuation, special symbols, or numbers, if they are not needed for your analysis.

3. **Tokenization**:
   - **Word Tokenization**: Split the text into individual words. This is the most common form of tokenization and can be done using spaces and punctuation as delimiters.
   - **Subword Tokenization**: Break words into smaller units, such as prefixes, suffixes, or even characters. This is useful for handling unknown words or languages with complex morphology.
   - **Sentence Tokenization**: Divide the text into sentences. This is often a preliminary step before word tokenization.

4. **Handling Edge Cases**:
   - Address contractions (e.g., "don't" to "do" and "not") and compound words.
   - Manage special cases like hyphenated words or abbreviations.

5. **Post-processing**:
   - **Stemming**: Reduce words to their base or root form (e.g., "running" to "run").
   - **Lemmatization**: Similar to stemming, but more sophisticated, reducing words to their dictionary form (e.g., "better" to "good").

6. **Output Tokens**:
   - The final output is a list or array of tokens that can be used for further processing, such as analysis, modeling, or natural language processing tasks.
